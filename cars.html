Cars

An automobile, commonly known as a car, is a usually four-wheeled vehicle designed primarily for passenger transportation. It is commonly propelled by an internal-combustion engine using a volatile fuel1. Cars have become the primary means of family transportation worldwide, with an estimated 1.4 billion in operation. They consist of thousands of component parts, evolving from breakthroughs in technology like electronic computers, high-strength plastics, and new alloys.
